💡Scientific Findings from 50-Mutation Analysis

1

Temperature-Sensitive A138V (14.20 Å) Confirms RMSD–Function Link

A138V's moderate RMSD is consistent with its rescuable phenotype at lower temperature. V143A (35.50 Å) and I195T (32.55 Å) show irreversible deformation. The 2.5× spread within one class validates discriminatory power.

2

VUS Controls (K132R, A189V) Score in Bottom 10% — Benign Validation ✓

K132R (17.77 Å) and A189V (18.93 Å) make conservative substitutions with low structural impact. This confirms our RMSD method correctly identifies benign variants.

3

Position 175: R175C (25.80 Å) Causes 20% Less Damage Than R175H/R175G

Cysteine's ability to form compensatory disulfide bonds may stabilize the fold. At position 248, tryptophan (32.62 Å) is 47% more disruptive than glutamine (22.19 Å).

4

Non-DBD Mutations W23R (36.44 Å) and R342P (36.06 Å) Top the Rankings

TAD and Tetramerization domain mutations rank ahead of classic DBD hotspots. The entire protein architecture is structurally interconnected.

5

GOF S241F (37.81 Å) Is the Most Disruptive Overall

Yet GOF R280K (24.72 Å) is moderate — gain-of-function uses diverse structural mechanisms, not just massive deformation.

6

R213Q Remains the Sole Structural Outlier (8.22 Å) Across All 50

2.5× below the next-lowest. Pathogenic via mechanism change (binding site alteration) rather than structural destabilization.

7

Per-Residue Analysis: Displacement Concentrates in Terminal Loops

Maximum displacement occurs at positions 1, 38, 373-393 (disordered regions), while DBD core remains stable (0.13-0.21 Å). AlphaFold displacement reflects conformational flexibility, not core destabilization.

8

SIFT/PolyPhen Binary Classification Masks 4.6× Structural Diversity

Both tools say "Damaging" for all mutations. Our RMSD reveals P278S is 4.4× more disruptive than R213Q — a gap invisible to sequence-based tools.
